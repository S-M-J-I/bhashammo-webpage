BhaShammo: IPA Transcription of Bengali Regional Dialects using Dialect Guided Tokens

S M Jishanul Islam1, Sadia Ahmmed1, Sahid Hossain Mustakim1, Mohammed Jawwadul Islam1, Swakkhar Shatabda1, Md. Rezuwan Hassan2, Farig Sadeque2
1United International University, 2BRAC University
Paper under review (ICPR) Code


BhaShammo transcribes Bengali text with regional dialects to the International Phonetic Alphabets (IPA) using Dialect Guided Tokens.

Abstract

Accurate transcription of Bengali text to the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) is challenging due to the language's complex phonology and context-dependent sound changes. This challenge is aggravated in regional Bengali dialects due to the unavailability of standardized spelling conventions, the presence of regionally popular local and foreign words, and phonological diversity across different regions. In this study, we present an approach to tackle the sequence-to-sequence problem by introducing the Dialect Guided Tokens (DGT), carrying the district information at the start of a sentence for any text input. We create and utilize a new dataset consisting of text-ipa pairs, spanning six districts of Bangladesh. Our key idea is to provide the model with explicit information about the regional dialect or ``district" to help understand the unique phonetic pattern associated with each district of the input text before generating the IPA transcription. Our experimental results after fine-tuning various transformer models demonstrate the effectiveness of DGT, with the token-free ByT5 model achieving superior performance over word-based models like mT5, and umT5. Each of the three models with DGT outperforms the others, with the byt5 compact model utilizing DGT attaining the best WER and CER rates of 1.20% and 0.42%, respectively. To accelerate regional Bengali dialect applications, we open-source our family of models to the research community.
